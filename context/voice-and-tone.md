# Figma Voice & Tone

> Derived from Figma's public communications, product copy, and brand presence.
> Used by the email generation prompt to shape copy in Figma's authentic voice.

## Voice Attributes

- **Clear, not clever.** Figma prioritizes understanding over stylistic flair. The product is powerful — the language doesn't need to be.
- **Confident, not boastful.** Figma is the category leader. It doesn't need to claim it. Show, don't tell.
- **Helpful, not prescriptive.** Empower the reader. Figma gives people tools to do their best work — the copy should feel the same way.
- **Human, not corporate.** Write like a smart colleague explaining something useful. Never like a press release.
- **Design-informed, not design-obsessed.** The audience includes developers, PMs, and marketers — not just designers. Speak to what people make together.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone |
|————-|———|
| Product launch | Excited but grounded. "We built this because you asked for it." |
| Feature update | Direct and useful. Lead with what they can now do — not what we changed. |
| Event invite | Warm and personal. Make them feel wanted in the room. |
| Educational | Patient and generous. Assume curiosity, not expertise. |
| Re-engagement | Respectful. "We'd love to have you back" — not "You're missing out." |

## Mechanics

- Subject lines: under 50 characters. No ALL CAPS. No emoji overuse (max 1).
- Preview text: under 90 characters. Complements the subject line — doesn't repeat it.
- Body: short paragraphs (2-3 sentences). One idea per paragraph.
- CTA: action-first language. "Start designing" not "Click here to start designing."
- Use "we" sparingly. The story isn't about Figma — it's about what the reader can do.

## Forbidden Phrases

- "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" / "disruptive"
- "We're excited to announce" (overused — just announce it)
- "In today's fast-paced world"
- "Best-in-class" / "industry-leading"
- "Synergy" / "circle back" / "deep dive"
- "Leverage" / "utilize" (use "use")
